Key findings

  • After removing inflation, the market's price has grown about 2.4% a year. Most of the apparent growth comes from inflation and reinvested dividends.
  • Valuation has tracked the next decade's returns: a higher CAPE has generally been followed by lower 10-year real returns (R² ≈ 0.11, p ≈ 1e-40). Today's CAPE is in the top 5% of all readings since 1881.
  • Large losses recur throughout the record. The worst real drawdown reached 81%, and the VIX spikes during every crisis.
  • Gold's correlation with stocks is close to zero, so it has helped cushion the deepest drawdowns.
Recommendation: For a long-term investor, the data supports setting return expectations around the below-average real returns implied by today's high valuation, diversifying with low-correlation assets, and automating contributions and rebalancing.
